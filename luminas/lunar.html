Why Anorthosite

The Connection Between
Greenland and the Moon

The lunar highlands are dominated by anorthosite — a rock composed almost entirely of plagioclase feldspar. Greenland’s White Mountain deposit shares this rare geochemistry, making it the premier terrestrial source for lunar simulant feedstock.

01
Same Mineralogy as Lunar Highlands

Dominated by plagioclase feldspar — the mineral that constitutes over 90% of the lunar highland crust. The most geologically accurate simulant feedstock available on Earth, validated by comparison with Apollo sample compositions.

02
Accurate Iron-Poor Chemistry

Exceptionally low Fe₂O₃ levels accurately reflect the iron-poor anorthositic composition of the lunar highlands — critical for matching optical albedo, thermal properties, and spectral signatures used in remote sensing calibration.

03
Zero Toxic By-Products

Unlike many industrial minerals, anorthosite processing generates no hazardous waste streams, making it safe for closed-loop ISRU system testing, pressurized habitat simulations, and long-term laboratory environments.

04
ISRU Feedstock Potential

Anorthosite is a primary candidate for in-situ resource utilization on the Moon. Its high aluminum and calcium content enables potential oxygen extraction, metal refining, and construction material production in lunar surface scenarios.

05
Consistent, Scalable Supply

Unlike meteorite-derived analogs, Greenland anorthosite is available in industrial quantities — from gram-scale laboratory samples to multi-tonne research consignments — without supply constraints or export restrictions.
