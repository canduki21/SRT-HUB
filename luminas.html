The Lunar Connection

Why Anorthosite for
Moon Research?

Greenland’s White Mountain deposit yields the most mineralogically accurate terrestrial analog to lunar highland regolith available on Earth.

01
Same Mineralogy as Lunar Highlands

Dominated by plagioclase feldspar — the mineral that constitutes over 90% of the lunar highland crust. The most geologically accurate simulant feedstock available on Earth.

02
Zero Toxic By-Products

Unlike many industrial minerals, anorthosite processing generates no hazardous waste streams, making it safe for closed-loop ISRU system testing and long-term lab use.

03
Low Iron Content

Exceptionally low Fe₂O₃ levels accurately reflect the iron-poor anorthositic composition of the lunar highlands — critical for optical and thermal property matching.

04
Sustainably Sourced

Mined from the White Mountain deposit in Nuuk, Greenland under strict environmental standards with minimal ecological impact and long-term deposit stewardship.

Applications

Versatile Across Sectors

Anorthosite’s unique chemistry — high aluminum, low silica, zero toxic by-products — serves both space science and advanced terrestrial industry.

01
Lunar Studies & ISRU

Premier terrestrial feedstock for lunar highland simulants — used in ISRU experiments, planetary surface research, and astronaut training scenarios.

02
E-Glass Fiber

Delivers superior electrical insulation with a reduced environmental footprint for aerospace, automotive, and construction sectors.

03
Paints & Coatings

Reduces VOC emissions while improving coating durability and performance across architectural and industrial applications.

04
Friction Materials

Strengthens phenolic friction resins in high-performance brake systems for automotive and aerospace applications.

05
Polymers & Plastics

Enhances anti-block properties, UV stability, flame resistance, and structural toughness in polymer compounds.

06
Mineral Wool

High aluminum and calcium content makes Greenspar an ideal raw material for mineral wool and thermal insulation manufacturing.
