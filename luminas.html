The Lunar Connection

Why Anorthosite for
Moon Research?

Greenland's White Mountain deposit yields some of the most mineralogically accurate terrestrial analogs to lunar highland regolith available anywhere on Earth.

🌑
Same Mineralogy as Lunar Highlands

Dominated by plagioclase feldspar — the mineral that makes up over 90% of the lunar highland crust — making it the most geologically accurate simulant feedstock available on Earth.

♻️
Zero Toxic By-Products

Unlike many industrial minerals, anorthosite processing generates no hazardous waste, making it safe for closed-loop ISRU system testing and long-term lab use.

🏔️
Sustainably Sourced

Mined from the White Mountain deposit, Nuuk, Greenland under strict environmental standards with minimal ecological impact.

Mineral Composition
Plagioclase Feldspar≥ 90%
Aluminum Oxide (Al₂O₃)~27%
Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂)~50%

Applications

Versatile Across Sectors

Anorthosite's unique chemistry — high aluminum, low silica, no toxic by-products — serves both space science and advanced terrestrial industry.

🌕
Lunar Studies & ISRU

Premier terrestrial analog for lunar highland regolith — used in ISRU experiments, lunar surface research, and astronaut training scenarios.

🔬
E-Glass Fiber

Delivers superior electrical insulation with a reduced environmental footprint for aerospace, automotive, and construction sectors.

🎨
Paints & Coatings

Greenspar products reduce VOC emissions while improving coating performance and durability in demanding environments.

⚙️
Friction Materials

Strengthens phenolic friction resins in high-performance brake systems for automotive and aerospace applications.

🧪
Polymers & Plastics

Enhances anti-block properties, UV stability, flame resistance, and structural toughness in polymer compounds.

🏗️
Mineral Wool

High aluminum and calcium content makes Greenspar an ideal raw material for mineral wool and thermal insulation.
