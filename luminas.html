About Lumina

Pioneering Sustainable Materials

A pioneering material science company addressing today’s significant manufacturing challenges with unparalleled sustainability.

"We are a pioneering material science company addressing today’s significant manufacturing challenges with unparalleled sustainability."

Lumina crafts specialized products through tailored chemistry and processing solutions, operating primary mining facilities 80 km west of Kangerlussuaq on Greenland’s west coast.

Our anorthosite — marketed under the Greenspar brand — is the only commercially available high-purity anorthosite product in the world, offering a sustainable alternative to conventional industrial minerals across e-glass, coatings, polymers, friction materials, and space research.

With no toxic by-products and an exceptionally low environmental footprint, Lumina is uniquely positioned at the intersection of industrial materials science and planetary exploration.

2013
Exploration Begins

Lumina’s journey initiated with exploration activities at the White Mountain anorthosite deposit, located 80 km west of Kangerlussuaq on Greenland’s west coast.

2017
Construction at Qaqortorsuaq

Construction commenced at the Qaqortorsuaq site in Greenland, establishing the primary mining infrastructure and processing facilities.

2019
First Mineral Shipment

First mineral shipment successfully completed, marking Greenland’s entry into the global specialty minerals market. Director of Operations Peter Madsen’s fourth mining venture in Greenland.

Now
Global Supply Chain

Lumina operates a fully vertically integrated supply chain spanning three continents, with mining in Greenland, advanced processing in Jeffersonville, Indiana (USA), and operations in Mexico and Europe.

Lunar Research

Why Anorthosite for Moon Research?

Greenland’s White Mountain deposit yields the most mineralogically accurate terrestrial analog to lunar highland regolith available on Earth.

01
Same Mineralogy as Lunar Highlands

Dominated by plagioclase feldspar — the mineral that constitutes over 90% of the lunar highland crust. The most geologically accurate simulant feedstock on Earth.

02
Zero Toxic By-Products

Processing generates no hazardous waste streams, making it safe for closed-loop ISRU system testing and long-term lab use.

03
Low Iron Content

Exceptionally low Fe₂O₃ levels accurately reflect the iron-poor anorthositic composition of the lunar highlands — critical for optical and thermal property matching.

04
Sustainably Sourced

Mined in Nuuk, Greenland under strict environmental standards with minimal ecological impact and long-term deposit stewardship.

Applications

Versatile Across Sectors

Anorthosite’s unique chemistry — high aluminum, low silica, zero toxic by-products — serves both space science and advanced terrestrial industry.

01
Lunar Studies & ISRU

Premier terrestrial feedstock for lunar highland simulants — used in ISRU experiments, planetary surface research, and astronaut training scenarios.

02
E-Glass Fiber

Delivers superior electrical insulation with a reduced environmental footprint for aerospace, automotive, and construction sectors.

03
Paints & Coatings

Reduces VOC emissions while improving coating durability and performance across architectural and industrial applications.

04
Friction Materials

Strengthens phenolic friction resins in high-performance brake systems for automotive and aerospace applications.

05
Polymers & Plastics

Enhances anti-block properties, UV stability, flame resistance, and structural toughness in polymer compounds.

06
Mineral Wool

High aluminum and calcium content makes Greenspar an ideal raw material for mineral wool and thermal insulation manufacturing.
