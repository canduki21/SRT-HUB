A Versatile Mineral
Across Sectors

Anorthosite's unique geochemistry — high aluminum, low silica, no toxic by-products — makes it an ideal material across both terrestrial industry and space science.

🌕

Lunar Studies & ISRU

Anorthosite from Greenland's White Mountain is compositionally and mineralogically analogous to lunar highland regolith, making it the premier terrestrial simulant feedstock for lunar surface research, in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) experiments, and astronaut training scenarios.

Why Anorthosite

The Lunar
Connection

Greenland's White Mountain anorthosite is among the most compositionally accurate terrestrial analogs to lunar highland regolith — making it the material of choice for space agencies and research institutions.

🌑
Lunar Highland Analog

Composed predominantly of plagioclase feldspar — the same mineral that dominates lunar highland crust — making it the most geologically accurate simulant feedstock available.

⚡
Zero Toxic By-Products

Unlike many industrial minerals, anorthosite processing produces no hazardous waste streams, making it safe for closed-loop ISRU system testing.

♻️
Sustainably Sourced

Mined under strict environmental standards in Greenland with a commitment to minimal ecological impact and long-term deposit stewardship.

Mineral Composition
Plagioclase Feldspar ≥ 90%
Aluminum Oxide (Al₂O₃) ~27%
Calcium Oxide (CaO) ~14%
Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂) ~50%
Quartz (free SiO₂) < 1%
Iron Oxide (Fe₂O₃) < 0.5%
